    The New School’s Vera List Center for Art and Politics funded a year-long project called the Art and Social Justice Working Group, in which a group of scholars, activists, and community organizers compiled materials that explored various themes on the subject of art and activism. The resulting website (this website) is a diverse repository of information that seeks to broaden the parameters of how art can be inflected with the goal of advancing social justice, and the site itself seems to me a particularly beautiful (albeit not so graceful) iteration on a wiki structure.
    http://www.veralistcenter.org/art-and-social-justice/

    In my undying love for excellent copy, I feel compelled to include bidoun.org, which structures its large archive of articles through a fairly intuitive and somewhat unconventional series of cascading links. Information is easy to find, well-organized, and the design of the site looks relevant—muted but not conservative.
