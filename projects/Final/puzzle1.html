Alternative Text

The frame is an abstraction used to structure and organize meaning. In mass communication theory, “To frame is to select some aspects of a perceived reality and make them more salient in a communicating text…” (Entman, 52). While “at the same time, people’s information processing and interpretation are influenced by preexisting meaning structures or schemas” (Scheufele, 3). So while facts are born neutral, they inevitably become framed and politicized when disseminated. Without scrutiny, truth can rapidly morph into falsehoods framed as truth by a specific agenda. In the age of media bubbles, cognitive dissonance strengthens specific frames, and the line between facts and alternative facts becomes dangerously blurred.
